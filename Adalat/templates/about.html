The insipiration to develop this idea Introduction

Justice will not be served until those who are unaffected are as outraged as those who are.

~ Benjamin Franklin

Access to justice has become an important issue in many justice systems around the world. Increasingly, technology is seen as a potential facilitator of access to justice, particularly in terms of improving justice sector efficiency.

Problem Existing System

HCs have 5.1 million, lower courts have 34 million cases pending: Law Ministry.

Reason Paperwork

Every year, over 60,000 cases are presented before the Supreme Court of India. Each document in each case has to be filed in a specific format. For example, each document can only be typed in a 13-14 font size. They must also have a 3cm margin, be double-spaced and should be printed on ONE side of the paper.

Proposed System

- Incorporating Technology
To maintain digital record of cases to reduce offline work and hassle of judges to deal with paper works.

- E-Courts
To present evidences like digital presence of person to record statement in case of inability of making physical presence to fast track proceedings.

- Increasing Accountability
Making court proceeding live or presenting recorded sessions online to make public aware of inside happenings of court rooms and making proceedings more accountable.
